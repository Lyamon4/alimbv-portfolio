About

I'm Alim, 14 years old. I study at the Republican Physics and Mathematics School (RFMSH) in Kazakhstan.

I started coding at 12 out of curiosity: I wanted to see whether I could build something that actually works. My background in competitive math taught me to understand the idea, find a solution, and carry it through. Programming felt the same, except the result could become a real product.

I learned that code alone isn't enough. A product also needs a real problem and a clear understanding of the people using it. That is what pulled me toward AI and startups.

I built Prism, an AI-powered student wellbeing platform, and Goalden, an AI coach that makes progress the price of scrolling.

Outside of code, I'm into basketball, planes, AI startups, LARP, and electronic music.
