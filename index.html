About

I'm Alim, 13 years old. I study at the Republican Physics and Mathematics School (RFMSH).

I started coding at 12, just out of curiosity — I wanted to understand if I could build something that actually works. Then I discovered AI, startups, and young founders who were already building products at 15–17. At some point I thought: why not me?

I came to IT through a love of problems. In competitive math, I always liked that you can't just memorize the answer — you have to understand the idea, find the solution, and carry it through. Programming felt the same, except here you can turn the result into a real product that real people use.

Over time I realized code alone isn't enough. You can build strong technology, but if nobody needs it, it's not a product. That's why startups hooked me. You have to understand people, their problems, and their needs — not just write code.

My first project is Prism, an AI-powered digital ecosystem for monitoring and supporting students' psycho-emotional wellbeing. Right now it's being piloted at RFMSH.

I also built Goalden — an AI coach that doesn't let you scroll until you show real progress. When you open YouTube, TikTok, Instagram or Reddit, it asks: “What did you do on the task you promised to finish?” Real answers only. If the progress is genuine, Goalden opens the feed for 15 minutes. Then the door closes again. For me, it's not just a blocker. It's a cash register before dopamine: pay with progress first, then scroll.

Outside of code, I'm into basketball, planes, AI startups, LARP, and electronic music.
